Why we explore!

Nothing inspires human curiosity more than what lurks beyond the visible realm of our skies! Recent advancements in technology have made it so that we can see, and indeed reach, places in space that we never knew existed. This page will help you understand the history behind space travel, including what we know, what we think we know, and what we'd like to find out (like, are there aliens up there!?) We’ll pay tribute to the brilliant and brave people who embarked upon the great wilderness in the sky before us, and we’ll also review current events in space travel, as well as projects that our great space engineers in the US and abroad hope to undertake one day! H ave fun exploring our page, and we hope it inspires you to read, watch, and learn more about space exploration! Oh yeah, and scroll down a bit to get to know the Space Cadets who made this page possible.

Meet the Space Cadets!

Dr. Jane Owen

Dr. Owen earned her Ph.D. in Rocket Science at Davidson University before joining NASA to oversee its Domestic Space Rocket Engineering Program. She is responsible for crucial innovations in rocket science, including NASA’s proprietary temperature modulation system. Throughout her career, she’s mentored several generations of rocket scientists and presently serves as a guest lecturer at the MIT Kavil Institute. In her free time, Dr. Owen volunteers as an archivist at the New York Public Library Space Center.

Dr. Monika Davis

Dr. Davis is an Aeronautics Engineer and an Expert Planetary Cartographer. She achieved worldwide fame in 1996 by using a hobby telescope on her back porch to analyze and re-map the Orion Constellation. Her paper titled “Oh no, Oh-rion!” revolutionized space cartography and resulted in various invitations to lecture at elite astronomy conferences. Presently, Monika serves as an Aeronautics Consultant to the United States Space Force.

Dr. Alex Taft

Dr. Taft began his career as a Theoretical Astrophysicist. In 2014, after years of studying Matter-Antimatter Asymmetry, he was awarded the Fields Medal for his landmark paper titled “Barry-o-what?”, in which he challenged the key tenants of Baryogenesis, including a scathing rejection of the Sakharov Condition of thermal non-equilibrium. Since then, Dr. Taft has produced a voluminous collection of books challenging conventional space theory. His newest one, A People’s History of Space, will be available in June 2026.

Dr. Ro Dodemaide

After an illustrious career at NASA, during which she piloted 14 successful space missions, Dr. Dodemaide was recruited to serve as a General in the Space Force. She is presently the lead designer on a Federal Coalition to develop a Space Force Station on Planet Mars. In her spare time, Dr. Dodemaide enjoys writing about the history of space travel. Her authorized biography of Buzz Aldrin, Buzz, was nominated for a National Book Award.

Evan Weiss

Evan is the host of the Space Exploration 101 podcast, a platform for space theorists, scientists, and astronauts to discuss complex theories of space travel in common terms. His amateur theorizing on space travel can be read on his Substack, Spaced Out.
