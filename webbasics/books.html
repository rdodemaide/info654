The Murderbot Diaries

A depressed human/machine construct breaks its' governing code so it can watch lots of crappy tv, and hates that it has to keep working and dealing with humans. These books are hilarious, quick reads and I recommend them constantly.

Broken Earth Series

The world building in the Broken Earth books is jaw dropping and I honestly think these are some of the best books ever written. The Fifth Season knocked my socks off, and the entire series is just amazing. It has Avatar: The Last Airbender vibes but much, much darker - orogenes, who can control rocks/minerals/earth, are extremely necessary & extremely oppressed.

Wayward Children Series

This series is in a universe where some kids who don't fit in are lucky enough to find doorways to worlds they do belong in, but unfortunately sometimes they eventually get spat back out. Those returned kids end up in a boarding school to help them deal and try to make their way back home.

A Wizard's Guide to Defensive Baking

A very fun book - how to survive being targeted for being a wizard when your magic is limited to dough!

The Scholomance Trilogy

Trying to survive wizard school is not easy when monsters are always trying to eat you and you constantly have to fight your natural talent for ending the world. A fun series that will make you rethink wanting to be a wizard as a teen.

The Folk of the Air Series

Fairies can be pretty evil so be very careful trying to navigate life in the Fairie realm as a human! These are quick and very enjoyable reads, I've reread them a few times and love it every time.

My Heart is a Chainsaw

Jade is the town outcast, and she is obsessed with horror movies. This makes her uniquely qualified to spot the warning signs when her small town is turning into a real-life horror movie, but no one wants to listen. Jade feels so real and reminds me of myself as a teenager in some ways, and I wish I could just give her a hug.

The Imperial Radch Series

Breq is the last ancillary soldier remaining from a destroyed war ship, and is seeking revenge. I love this series and have reread it many times, but I hesitate to recommend it now because no one I've recommended it to has been able to get into it enough to finish the first book even.

The Locked Tomb Series

Necromancers in space solving a murder mystery while trying to become immortal! These books are hilarious and I love the world building. The second book, Harrow the Ninth, makes no sense until you figure out whats going on later in the book, but on reread I loved it. The final book in the series seems to be on hiatus unfortunately, but these are still very much worth reading in the meantime.

Black Sun

Inspired by Native American cultures and religions, the world building in Black Sun is amazing. A boy becomes the incarnation of the crow god so he can get the revenge his mother wanted, and war is ready to break out in a city shared by several tribes. This is the first book in the Between Earth & Sky series, but unfortunately I didn't find the other books to be as good.
