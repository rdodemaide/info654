My Top 10 Favorite Authors

  1. Martha Wells
  2. N. K. Jemisin
  3. T. Kingfisher
  4. Ann Leckie
  5. Tamsyn Muir
  6. Holly Black
  7. Naomi Novik
  8. Stephen Graham Jones
  9. Seanan McGuire
  10. Rebecca Roanhorse

Martha Wells

Martha Wells wrote my favorite book series - The Murderbot Diaries. She also wrote Witch King, another of my favorite books, and has a number of other books I haven't read yet. I've been avoiding these partly on purpose so that I still have books of hers that I'll get to read for the first time.

Click here for Martha's website.

N. K. Jemisin

N. K. Jemisin reignited my passion for reading when my interest in it was starting to drag. She has written several series, and I've absolutely loved all of them and have reread them all at least a few times.

Click here for N. K.'s website.

T. Kingfisher

T. Kingfisher is a prolific writer, writing both Fantasy and Horror. This is a pen name she uses for these genres - she also writes children's books under her real name, Ursula Vernon. Her books always suck me right in, and she wrote the only book (The Twisted Ones) that I've had to put aside for a while because it was too scary.

Click here for T.'s website.

Ann Leckie

Ann Leckie doesn't have many books, but I absolutely love her Imperial Radch series and think she creates the most fascinating worlds.

Click here for Ann's website.

Tamsyn Muir

Tamsyn Muir also doesn't have many books, but I'm obsessed with her Locked Tomb series. Her sense of humor kills me and I really hope she writes more.

Click here for Tamsyn's website.

Holly Black

Holly Black usually writes about the worlds of humans and fairies clashing, and I always find her books to be a lot of fun. I'm still working my way through her books, and am always excited to get started on one.

Click here for Holly's website.

Naomi Novik

Naomi Novik writes books and short stories that stay in the back of my head and I think back on constantly. I'm also a fan of her for her involvement in the creation of Archive of Our Own (AO3).

Click here for Naomi's website.

Stephen Graham Jones

Stephen Graham Jones is a Horror writer, and everything of his that I've read has been a gut punch in the best way possible. There are lines from his books that I think about all the time because they painted such a strong, clear picture that I can't shake.

Click here for Stephen's website.

Seanan McGuire

Seanan McGuire is an astoundingly prolific writer, with several long running series that have new releases most years. She also writes under the name Mira Grant for her horror books that have a kind of biological or medical angle to them. I happened to be reading her Newsflesh trilogy when COVID hit and parts of it were eerily similar to what was going on, so she had clearly done her virology homework well.

Click here for Seanan's website.

Rebecca Roanhorse

Rebecca Roanhorse creates some of the most fascinating worlds in her books, and I think is one of the best writers in SFF for fight scenes.

Click here for Rebecca's website.
